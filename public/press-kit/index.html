About Plain Act

Plain Act publishes quiet, practical management manuals for people carrying authority under pressure. Its work focuses on restraint, decision timing, communication, feedback, delegation, and building calm authority without motivational noise.

Plain Act creates practical management books and business content about work, leadership, decisions, responsibility, execution, team pressure, and the real behavioral patterns that shape performance.

Official Book

Title

The First 30 Days as a New Manager

A Practical Manual for First-Time Managers on Authority, Feedback, Delegation, and Decision Timing

Short Description

Plain Act publishes quiet management manuals for people carrying authority under pressure. Its first book, The First 30 Days as a New Manager, helps first-time managers build authority, give feedback, delegate, and make decisions without overreacting or overexplaining.

Book Summary

The First 30 Days as a New Manager is a practical day-by-day execution manual for first-time managers, newly promoted team leads, and supervisors facing real workplace pressure.

Instead of motivational leadership theory, it gives one focused action per day across authority, feedback, delegation, communication, decision timing, meetings, team rhythm, and trust repair.
