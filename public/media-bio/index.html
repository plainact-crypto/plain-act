Standard Media Bio

Plain Act publishes quiet, practical management manuals for people carrying authority under pressure. Its work focuses on restraint, decision timing, communication, feedback, delegation, and building calm authority without motivational noise.

Plain Act’s first book, The First 30 Days as a New Manager, is a day-by-day execution manual for first-time managers, newly promoted team leads, and supervisors learning how to stabilize their role, reduce unnecessary noise, and lead without overexplaining, overhelping, or reacting under pressure.

Long Bio

Plain Act is a management publishing brand built around a simple idea: authority under pressure requires restraint, clarity, and timing.

Instead of motivational leadership slogans, Plain Act creates practical manuals and cinematic business content for managers, team leads, supervisors, engineers, business professionals, and project teams dealing with real workplace responsibility.

Its first book, The First 30 Days as a New Manager, helps first-time managers avoid common early mistakes: overexplaining, staying too available, micromanaging after delegation, delaying feedback, and making reactive decisions when pressure rises.

Plain Act’s work is designed for people who need clear, usable management thinking without loud performance, fake success talk, or empty motivation.

Book Mention

The First 30 Days as a New Manager is a practical day-by-day manual for first-time managers, newly promoted team leads, and supervisors carrying authority before it feels natural.
